# 📊 Customer Retention & Cohort Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes e‑commerce transaction data using cohort analysis to understand how customer behavior evolves after their first purchase.

The analysis focuses on three key dimensions:

📈 Customer acquisition trends  
🔁 Retention patterns across cohorts  
💰 Revenue contribution and customer lifetime value

By grouping customers based on their first purchase month, we track engagement and spending behavior over time to identify opportunities to improve long‑term retention and revenue.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Customer retention is a critical driver of sustainable business growth.

Acquiring new customers is significantly more expensive than retaining existing ones, making it important to understand how customer engagement changes after the first purchase.

This project uses cohort analysis to answer key business questions:

• How many customers return after their first purchase?  
• How quickly do customers become inactive?  
• Which customer cohorts generate the most long‑term value?

---

## 📦 Dataset

This analysis uses the **Online Retail dataset**, which contains transactional data from a UK-based e‑commerce retailer.

Each row represents a product purchased within a transaction.

### Key Columns

- **InvoiceNo** – Transaction ID  
- **StockCode** – Product identifier  
- **Description** – Product name  
- **Quantity** – Number of items purchased  
- **InvoiceDate** – Timestamp of the transaction  
- **UnitPrice** – Price per item  
- **CustomerID** – Unique identifier for each customer  
- **Country** – Customer location

---

## 🔍 Analytical Framework

The analysis is divided into three main phases:

### Phase 1 — Customer Acquisition Analysis
Understand how many new customers join the platform over time.

### Phase 2 — Customer Retention (Cohort Analysis)
Analyze how many customers return to make purchases after their first purchase.

### Phase 3 — Revenue Retention / Customer Value
Examine how revenue evolves for each cohort and identify which cohorts generate the most value.

---

## 📈 Phase 1 — Customer Acquisition Analysis

### 🎯 Objective
Understand how many new customers join the platform each month and identify acquisition trends.

Tracking acquisition helps provide context for retention performance and reveals periods of growth or decline.

###💡 Key Insights
Customer acquisition peaked in December 2010, likely driven by holiday demand.

Acquisition declined through mid‑2011 before recovering later in the year.

Cohort sizes vary significantly across months, suggesting seasonal purchasing behavior or marketing influence.

## 🔁 Phase 2 — Customer Retention (Cohort Analysis)

### 🎯 Objective
Evaluate how many customers return to make purchases after their first transaction.

Customers are grouped by first purchase month (cohort) and their activity is tracked across subsequent months.

### 💡 Key Insights
- Customer retention drops sharply after the first month.

- Most cohorts retain approximately 20–40% of customers in Month 1.

- Early cohorts show slightly stronger long‑term retention compared to later cohorts.

This suggests that early engagement strategies may significantly impact repeat purchasing behavior.

## 💰 Phase 3 — Revenue Retention & Customer Value

### 🎯 Objective
Analyze how revenue generated by each cohort evolves over time.

This phase evaluates:

- Total revenue by cohort
- Average revenue per customer

to better understand long‑term customer value.

### 💡 Key Insights
- Early cohorts generate the highest cumulative revenue due to longer active periods.

- Revenue contribution declines over time as customer activity decreases.

- Average revenue per customer is highest shortly after acquisition, indicating customers spend most early in their lifecycle.

## Executive Summary

This analysis examined customer acquisition, retention, and revenue patterns using cohort analysis.

Customer acquisition was strongest at the beginning of the dataset and declined through mid‑2011 before recovering later in the year.

Retention analysis revealed that customer engagement drops significantly after the first purchase, with most cohorts retaining roughly 20–40% of customers after the first month.

Revenue analysis shows that the majority of customer value is generated in the early months after acquisition, highlighting the importance of encouraging repeat purchases shortly after a customer's first order.

Overall, improving early customer engagement and retention could significantly increase long‑term customer value.
